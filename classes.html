Jazz Drumming

Jazz drumming is the art of playing percussion in jazz styles ranging from 1910s-style Dixieland jazz to 1970s-era jazz fusion and 1980s-era Latin jazz.

Funk Drumming

Funk creates a groove by emphasizing the drummer's "feel and emotion", which including "occasional tempo fluctuations", the use of swing feel in some songs, and less use of fills.

BossaNova Drumming

The Bossa Nova bass line retains the characteristic Samba drum beat pattern. Having originated in Brazil, the clave is slightly different from the Afro Cuban Clave varieties.

Blues Drumming

The Blues describes the drummer's creation of a solid rhythmic foundation within the music so that other musicians can know exactly where the groove is.

Folk Drumming

Drums have always held a special place in folk music. They serve as the pulse, dictating the rhythm of its dances, communicating its stories, and preserving its traditions.

Metal Drumming

With roots in blues rock and psychedelic/acid rock drum playing, heavy metal drummers play with emphatic beats, and overall loudness using an aggressive performing style.

Reggae Drumming

Reggae is a 4/4 rhythm that with the main emphasis on the third beat, instead of typically emphasized second and fourth beats of most modern music. It is the distinctive third beat that gives it the special sound.

Rock Drumming

One of the defining features of rock drumming is its driving rhythms. The drummers in rock bands establish a solid foundation by emphasizing the backbeat, typically played on the snare drum.
